PRODUCTION DESIGN

Objective: Manage and execute design projects from internal requestors within a time sensitive company workflow while adhering to strict regulatory requirements.

Role: I served as a production artist by customizing design templates to meet the specific needs of interdepartmental requestors. Projects included the creation of workbooks, sales collateral, and signage. This role not only required knowledge of Adobe products and attention to detail, but also highly developed project management skills. Each project went through a three tier approval process including a peer review, manager review, and final approval. The final approval required written authorization from the requestor, the Branding Department Director, a Clinical Development manager, and often an individual from the Legal Department and Senior Management. Additionally, each one of these approval stages usually contained several rounds of communication with the reviewer where “redlines,” as a commented PDF of the design, were submitted back to me as the designer to implement new copy or positioning. Once approved by all members, I printed out each iteration of the design, all redlines, all signed approvals, a proof of the final draft, and handed it over to the Associate Production Design Manager for an additional final review before submitting the packet to the Document Control Department. To begin production, I then submitted a print request to our in-house print shop for the requested amount of brochures, booklets, signs, etc. Depending on the complexity of the project, this step also required follow-up communication with the Print Production Team to discuss prepress, imposition, bindery and delivery concerns. As a final step, I updated digital versions of the collateral to match the new revision and also updated the online catalog. I often worked on several of these production design projects at once, each with their own deadline which was frequently event-driven. Through these projects I developed strong time management, multi-tasking, and communication skills while also executing on and improving my design technique.
