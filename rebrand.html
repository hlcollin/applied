FLOOR GRAPHIC INSTALLATION
CORPORATE REBRAND STRATEGY

Objective: Create a company-wide system to support and maintain Applied Medical's new corporate brand identity while ensuring collateral pieces can be designed in an efficient manner.

Role: I worked with a team of designers and operations specialists to analyze the needs, pain points, and inefficiencies of Applied Medical’s current design process and identified how these impacted the communication of the company’s brand identity both internally and externally.

Problem: We found that when designers were commissioned to create a new product brochure for a medical device, they were navigating through the complex server file structure in order to find older InDesign Documents that contained design elements (tables, image containers, paragraph sytles) that they wanted to use, then copy and pasting these elements into their new working document. Not only was this process time consuming, it was also dangerous. Because the designers were pasting design elements with old product information and then replacing the text with new copy, there was a risk they could overlook a piece of text from another product and thereby create innacuracies in the document. In the medical device industry, inaccuracies and typos are cause for alarm. Furthermore, by copy and pasting old design elements, the consistency of the newly rebranded product brochures was quickly degrading. Like the game of "telephone," each iteration of copy and pasting introduced new changes from the original design.

Solution: Based on our findings, our team developed and implemented a comprehensive solution utilizing the capabilities of a data asset management system in conjunction with Adobe InDesign snippet functionality. We created a library of InDesign "snippets," or design elements, that could be accessed from censhare, a digital asset management system. These snippets contained the paragraph styles, image containers, and tables that the designers needed but were filled with placeholder text instead of product information. These snippets could be easily found in one centralized location and then dragged and dropped into the designer's working file. We conducted user testing with a handful of Applied Medical designers and found that the new system was faster and easier then what they were previously doing.
